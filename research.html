🔬 Research Studio

Inquiry-Based Research Project Builder

Great research starts with great questions — not answers. This studio guides you through a step-by-step inquiry process: explore a topic, craft research questions, gather evidence, and let your thesis emerge from the investigation.

Step 2 of 7

❓ Question Workshop

Skill: Questioning & Critical Thinking

Not all questions are equal. A strong research question is open-ended, specific enough to research, and leads to analysis rather than simple description.

❌ Thin Questions

  • Can be answered with a fact or date
  • "When did the Taiping Rebellion start?"
  • "Who led the Long March?"
  • "What was the Treaty of Nanjing?"

✅ Thick Questions

  • Require analysis, comparison, or evaluation
  • "To what extent was the Taiping Rebellion a product of Qing misgovernance?"
  • "How did the Long March transform the CCP's identity?"
  • "Why did China's response to the Opium Wars differ from Japan's?"

Craft a single, focused research question. It should be open-ended, arguable, and historically grounded.

🔍 Source Hunt

Skill: Source Evaluation & Evidence Gathering

For each sub-question, identify at least 2 sources that help answer it. Use the AHSAS Source Library or find your own. For each source, note whether it confirms, complicates, or contradicts what you expected.

📊 Evidence Matrix

Skill: Synthesis & Pattern Recognition

Organize your findings in a matrix: sub-question × source × key finding. Look for patterns, contradictions, and connections. What is the evidence telling you?

Step 6 of 7

💡 Emerging Thesis

Skill: Thesis Construction from Evidence

Based on everything you've learned, draft an arguable thesis statement. Your thesis should answer your research question and be supported by the evidence you gathered. Remember: a thesis is not a fact — it's an argument that others could disagree with.
