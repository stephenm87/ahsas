⚖️ Argumentation Targets

Refer to these exact criteria to ensure your contributions meet expected depth and complexity.

Arg: Claims

4 - Exceeding

Develops a concise, detailed historically defensible claim that uncovers a significant aspect of history.

3 - Meeting

Develops a detailed, historically defensible claim that establishes a coherent line of reasoning.

2 - Approaching

Develops an overly general or somewhat unclear historically defensible claim.

1 - Basic

Develops a historically defensible statement or observation.

Arg: Evidence

4 - Exceeding

Evidence is paraphrased with exceptional accuracy. Demonstrates nuanced understanding of source context leading to significant strengthening of claims.

3 - Meeting

Evidence varies, is paraphrased accurately and clearly, and effectively supports interpretation of the past.

2 - Approaching

Evidence is paraphrased with partial accuracy but may include errors or imprecise wording. Relevance is inconsistent.

1 - Basic

Evidence is inaccurately paraphrased, irrelevant, or does not effectively support interpretations of the past.

Arg: Reasoning

4 - Exceeding

Reasoning is consistently precise and well-developed. Skillfully corroborates evidence by comparing multiple sources.

3 - Meeting

Clear and logical explanations. Attempts to corroborate evidence by comparing multiple sources.

2 - Approaching

Reasoning is present but inconsistent, underdeveloped, or lacking precision. Corroboration is minimal or superficial.

1 - Basic

Struggles to explain how evidence supports a claim. Corroboration is not attempted or inaccurate.
